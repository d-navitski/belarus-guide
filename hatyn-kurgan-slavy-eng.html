Excursion: Memorial Complex “Khatyn” – Mound of Glory

Duration: 4 hours

Total distance: 140km

"We must exterminate population. It is our mission if we want to protect Germans. I have the right to annihilate millions of people of the lowest race who reproduce like worms..." — Hitler used to announce.

On March 30, 1941 during the Wehrmacht high command meeting Hitler pointed at the fact that the war against the Soviet Union would be a "war of annihilation", that this war "will differ considerably from the war in the West. In the East cruelty is too mellow for the future".

The policy was implemented…

On March 22, 1943 the village of Khatyn was burnt together with 149 inhabitants. But it wasn`t the only case, the village has become a symbol of sufferings of the civilian population during wartime as 185 other villages shared the same fate.

Although many villages, towns and cities were burnt to the ashes during the Great Patriotic War, our former big nation managed to rise to the top. The Mound of Glory which is 70m high symbolizes the feat of the Soviet Army and the place is especially important for Belarus as the victory in the battle here was another step on the way to freedom!
